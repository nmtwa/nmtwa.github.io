I built this board at home in the summer of '24.

1. I started with a 4'x8'x2" sheet of xps from Home Depot (the 1.5 lb/cuft density kind). I cut it into three 64"x24" pieces and glued them together with Elmer's glue.

![gluing]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/board_v1/gluing.jpeg)

2. Then I hotwired the blank in two steps: first rocker, then outline. I had some trouble with the wire creating channels in the blank. I think this might be solved by using a thinner wire with more tension.

![hotwire]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/board_v1/hotwire.jpeg)

3. I filled imperfections in the blank with some drywall putty we had laying around.

![fill_gaps]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/board_v1/fill_gaps.jpeg)

4. The next challenge was to add the track box. This part is extremely structurally important, so we need to add high density foam that goes from all the way from the bottom to the deck. First, I sawed out a hole to fill with high density pour foam. I routed out holes for the tracks, and glued them in with filled epoxy.
